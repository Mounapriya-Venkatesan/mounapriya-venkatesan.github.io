Phobos Proximity Operations — Formation GNC Feasibility Study

Independent Graduate Study · Rice University

Comprehensive feasibility analysis of sustained proximity operations around Phobos using Quasi-Satellite Orbits (QSOs) in the Mars–Phobos Hill frame — the orbit type used by JAXA's MMX mission. Phobos's sphere of influence (7.3 km) is smaller than its mean radius (11.3 km), making classical stable orbits impossible. This study derives QSO dynamics from Clohessy–Wiltshire equations, produces a full ΔV budget, and evaluates dual-spacecraft formation vs. single S/C architectures.

  • Identified QSO-30 (30 km amplitude) as optimal science orbit: 0.18 m/s/day SK, 18.7 km surface altitude, 7.65 h period — consistent with MMX mission baseline
  • Full 8-phase mission ΔV budget: 64.0 m/s → 14.6 kg propellant from 500 kg S/C (2.92% mass fraction)
  • Evaluated 3 hyperbolic flyby scenarios (e = 1.44–2.34, t_encounter = 69–222 min); dual formation (QSO-30 + QSO-50) recommended: 96% surface coverage vs. 42% flyby-only
  • Monte Carlo uncertainty analysis (N = 2,000, Phobos GM ±500 m³/s²): 15% contingency is 150× actual model uncertainty — identifies autonomous GNC as primary risk driver
  • Neural-network guidance augmentation identified as highest-value R&D direction, directly aligned with SFPL aerocapture GNC research
